## (a) THE PITCH SPINE — three lines, each with its graph anchor

Say these three, in this order. They are the spine of every artifact.

### SPINE 1 — "This is relational, not a knowledge graph."
The single sharpest differentiator the team ever drew.

- **The line:** *A knowledge graph is about knowledge. This is relational — it connects people,
  not catalogs one person's taglines.* Say it verbatim; it is already canon.
- **Anchors:** `claim:relational-not-knowledge-graph` · `article:differentiation-relational-and-crafted`
  (distills 8 nodes) · `flow:thread:product-wedge`.
- **The foil (use it out loud):** Eunice's "nodes" app. It is *just a graph of all the taglines in
  your life and doesn't connect to other people, so it doesn't hit* (`claim:eunice-nodes-misses`),
  and the UI *looks cheap — the predictable Obsidian knowledge-graph balls, weirdly gradient-shaded,
  with just regular words* (`claim:eunice-ui-cheap`). The Universe is the anti-Eunice: the nodes ARE
  people, the edges ARE real shared work and shared values, and Teri's skin is deliberately NOT the
  cheap-Obsidian-ball look (`gx/CLAUDE.md`: "NOT space themed... clean, glass-orb/spectrum").
- **Why it wins the judges:** the Neo4j prize wants a real semantic layer. "Relational not
  knowledge-graph" IS the palantir-level story — a typed ontology of Person→Person edges (WORKS_ON,
  SHARES_VALUE), not a bag of tags. The passport can only be written *because* the edges are real.

### SPINE 2 — "AI as an honest mirror."
The existential why underneath a party toy. This is what makes it more than a name-tag generator.

- **The line:** *Use AI to reflect an honest perspective back at yourself so you can make better
  choices* (`claim:solution-ai-honest-mirror`). At a party, the passport IS the mirror: it tells you
  who you are by telling you who, in this room, you'd click with and why.
- **Anchors:** `claim:solution-ai-honest-mirror` · `article:ai-as-honest-mirror` (8 nodes) ·
  `claim:existential-not-competency` · `claim:viewing-people-objectively-larger-context`.
- **The depth (one breath, only if you have the room):** among the materially comfortable the real
  problem isn't competency, it's existential — *why am I here, how do I relate to others*
  (`claim:existential-not-competency`). The mirror works because it *sees you objectively in life's
  larger context* (`claim:viewing-people-objectively-larger-context`). The party passport is the
  smallest, most fun version of that mirror.
- **Guard rail (do NOT skip):** the team itself flagged that empirically pinning down capricious
  relationships can feel *reductive and disturbing* (`claim:empirically-mapping-capricious-relationships`).
  So the passport must read as **recognition, not reduction** — a warm "here's who you'd love," never
  a cold "here is your file." This directly shapes the taste rules in (b).

### SPINE 3 — "Invisible AI: slide it in so it's not 'like AI'."
Johnny's mechanism. The reason the artifact is a *passport*, not a chatbot.

- **The line:** *Secretly slide AI in so it doesn't feel like AI* (`claim:johnny-angle-secretly-slide-ai`)
  — engineer it *subconsciously, NOT as an explicit chatbot / you-speaking-to-AI / AI-generating-reports*
  (`claim:engineer-subconsciously-not-explicit`).
- **Anchors:** `concept:invisible-ai` · `article:invisible-ai` (7 nodes) ·
  `claim:johnny-angle-secretly-slide-ai` · `claim:engineer-subconsciously-not-explicit`.
- **Why the passport is the perfect vessel:** *the moment you say "AI," people react*
  (`claim:the-moment-you-say-ai`), and *willingness to hand over info is the largest problem*
  (`claim:trust-is-the-gate`). A passport hides all of it: they fill a Luma form (never "talk to an
  AI"), and what comes back is a beautiful physical-feeling ticket. The graph traversal, the
  embedding clusters, the inference — invisible. The user experiences a gift, not a model.
- **The pitch payoff:** the AI is load-bearing (typed ontology gate, agent traversal, values
  embeddings) yet the user never once feels it. That gap IS invisible AI, demonstrated live.

## (b) MAGIC-INFERENCE TASTE RULES (G1) — "how does it know?" magical vs creepy

The magic-inference is the flip-side of the passport: *something they never told us* that makes them
go *"I didn't give this to AI — how does it know?"* (raw/0012, verbatim). Get the taste wrong and the
same feature becomes surveillance. These rules are the fence between delight and creep. Derived from
the invisible-AI + honest-mirror doctrine.

**RULE 1 — Infer only from what they GAVE. Never scrape-shock.**
The magic is *re-inference*, not new acquisition: *someone doesn't have to give a specific fact — we
take something and make it magical... infer something about them* (raw/0012). The delight comes from
recombining the Luma fields they willingly typed (school + major + what-they-do + values answer) into
something they didn't realize was legible — NOT from surfacing data they never handed over.
- GOOD: "You and three others all wrote that creativity means *changing the world* — you're the
  builders of the room." (recombination of the belief answer they gave)
- CREEPY / BANNED: anything that smells scraped — IG mutuals, their real followers, location history.
  Note: raw/0011 dreamed aloud about *extracting IG followers*; for the artifact that is the
  scrape-shock line — it violates `claim:trust-is-the-gate`. Stay inside the CSV.

**RULE 2 — Delight, not surveillance. It must feel like a gift, not a dossier.**
Proactive mapping is a *wow moment* that lowers hesitance *only when it feels impressive-for-you*
(`concept:proactive-relationship-mapping`). The tell: does the line make them smile and want to show a
friend, or check who else can see it? Frame every inference as a compliment or an invitation, never
an assessment. Recognition, not reduction (Spine 2's guard rail).

**RULE 3 — Validate the feeling first (the "dot" tone).**
The house tone for reflected-back inferences is warm-affirming before clever — validate who they are,
then reveal. A direction is only right if it *brings you closer to the vision's feeling*
(`claim:validate-direction-by-vision-feeling`); the mirror is meant to help you *make better choices*,
which lands only when it feels kind. So the magic line opens with warmth ("You're clearly the kind of
person who...") and *then* drops the specific inference. Cold-clever ("Statistically you cluster
with...") fails the taste test even when the inference is correct.

**RULE 4 — One inference, specific, falsifiable-feeling.**
One magic line per passport, concrete enough that it *could* have been wrong — that's what makes it
feel like it *knew*. Vague horoscope lines ("you love connecting with people") read as filler; a
specific bridge ("you should find the other person who left finance to make things") reads as magic.
This also satisfies the receipts audit: a specific inference traces to specific edges.

**RULE 5 — Never name the mechanism.** Invisible-AI law (`claim:engineer-subconsciously-not-explicit`):
the passport never says "our AI analyzed" or "based on embeddings." It just *knows*. The wow ("how
does it know?") dies the instant you explain the trick. Copy on the artifact is human-voiced, first-
person-to-the-reader, zero model vocabulary.

**RULE 6 — Every magic line is still relational.** Even the "hidden" inference points at a PERSON or a
shared human quality, per Spine 1. The magic isn't "you are an INTJ" (a tagline — that's Eunice); it's
"there's someone here who'd get the exact thing you're building." Inferences that terminate on the self
without connecting outward are off-thesis.

## (c) DEMO STORY BEATS (<60s) — mapped to judge psychology

Judges decide in the first ~15 seconds and remember the first and last thing (primacy + recency). The
wow must fire before 30s. NO setup, NO "so what we built is..." — open cold on the magic.

| t | Beat | On screen | Judge psychology |
|---|------|-----------|------------------|
| 0–8s | **Cold-open on the wow.** "This is a party for creatives. Everyone who signed up got THIS." Hold up / show a filled passport. | A single finished passport, beautiful, Teri-skinned. | **Primacy.** Lead with the artifact, not the architecture. They must *want* one before they know how it works. |
| 8–20s | **Sign up live.** Type one real name + the creativity belief into the form. Submit. | The Luma-style form → submit. | Live input = "this is real, not a mockup." Concreteness beats slides. |
| 20–32s | **The star ignites.** The new person appears in the Universe, edges snap to their shared-work + values cluster. | The graph animates the new node joining a glowing values-cloud. | **Wow-in-30s.** The relational graph — Spine 1 — made visible and alive. This is the Neo4j money shot. |
| 32–48s | **The passport writes itself.** Their passport renders: the 2 people to find + why (one same-work, one same-values), and the **magic inference** — "how does it know?" | Passport fills in, front then flip to the gradient back. | The payoff. Read the magic line out loud and let it land — that's the emotional peak. |
| 48–60s | **Land the spine + the ship.** "It's relational, not a knowledge graph — it connects people. The AI is invisible; they just got a passport. Live at [URL], deployed pipe, open repo." | URL + one-line architecture. | **Recency.** Last thing they hear = the three-word differentiator + proof it actually ships. |

**Rules for the run:** one real name, pre-seed the rest of the ~40-person test CSV so clusters already
glow (an empty graph has no wow). If check-in (G5) is cut, the two-state / passport-updates-on-check-in
line becomes ONE spoken roadmap sentence, not a live beat — never a dead button on stage.

**The one-breath version (memorize this):** *"Sign up to a party, and a passport writes itself — the
two people you'd click with tonight and one thing it somehow knew about you. It's relational, not a
knowledge graph, and the AI is completely invisible."*

## (d) SARAH'S MARKETING FRAME for the passport artifact

The passport artifact and the party/social-graph strand are **Sarah's** (attribution per raw/0011:
"party/social-graph strand is Sarah's"; the physical-artifact framing is hers in raw/0012). Frame it
in her terms.

- **The viral-ticket lineage.** The passport descends directly from the startup-school ticket that
  *went viral because everyone posted their dump ticket* (raw/0012). The frame: **"a ticket that went
  viral was just a flex. Yours knows your people."** Same shareable-artifact energy, but every
  passport is personalized and relational, so posting it says something true about you — built-in
  reason to share = built-in distribution.
- **A strong physical artifact for marketing.** Sarah explicitly wanted *a strong physical artifact
  for her marketing* (raw/0012). It photographs: front = your name, company, the two people to find +
  why, in clean black; flip = the hidden scavenger prompt + the **gradient backing**.
- **The gradient = the DNA-you (Sarah's idea).** *Your gradient from who you are — visually compare
  how you relate to other people* (raw/0012, Sarah's DNA-gradient idea, `gx` CLAUDE.md G2). Marketing
  angle: everyone's passport back is a unique gradient fingerprint; a wall of them is a wall of DNA.
  Collectible, comparable, screenshot-native.
- **The soft honest-mirror hook (why it's more than a party toy).** Under the fun, it's the same
  wedge Sarah & Teri drew: *not a "never miss a coffee chat" utility, but a tool for understanding
  your relationships and your story, kept for yourself* (`topic:understanding-your-relationships-and-story`,
  `concept:crm-vs-notion-database`). Party framing for launch; honest-mirror depth is the thesis it
  seeds. Don't lead with the depth — lead with the flex, let the depth be the afterglow.
- **Never say "AI" in the marketing copy.** `claim:the-moment-you-say-ai` — the moment you say AI,
  people react. The passport is sold as a *passport*, not an AI product. Invisible AI is a marketing
  law, not just an engineering one.

## (e) THE CURATION REFRAME (raw/0016 — supersedes the pass-1 problem framing, additively)

Johnny's story-seat yap #2, verbatim anchor `raw/0016`: *"People go to the events for the people.
The event is meant to curate for the people. but the way it currently works is that events dont
show anything about who the people will be… ITs onyl when you get to see your social grpah do you
know how its curated and when you bring your passport over itll show you what and how you can
create a more delightful experience for the process of going ot events."*

- **The problem now lives at CURATION-OPACITY:** events sell CTAs, never the people; the room was
  curated toward you and you never get to see it — before OR during.
- **The product sentence:** the graph = *seeing* the curation; the passport = *carrying* it into
  the room.
- **Two-state design is now thesis, not stretch-feature:** before = the curated-toward-you
  preview; during = live re-curation as people check in. (G5 remains cuttable as CODE; the line
  itself is now load-bearing pitch — if cut, it is the spoken roadmap sentence.)
- Pass-1 framings that survive: "trapped in your head" (as the during-cost), "three facts" (as the
  fix-was-already-in-the-form beat). "The wasted room" folds into curated-blind.
