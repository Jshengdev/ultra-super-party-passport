The Ultra Super Social Passport™ · positioning

Turn any guest list into a room that knows its people.

The Ultra Super Social Passport™ turns an event's guest list into a living relational map of the room — and hands every guest a passport: the two people they should find tonight, why, and one line about them it was never told. Drop a CSV; a passport writes itself.

The problem

Every event you've ever been to was curated — you just never got to see it.

Events sell the lineup, the venue, the ticket — never the people. A guest list of 200 says "software engineer" two hundred times and tells you nothing about which one you'd talk to for three hours. All the should-I-go-talk-to-them math happens alone, in your head — which is why big rooms are exhausting.

"I regret getting a passport for this party." Not because it knew my people. Not because it's cute as f•. Because I didn't have it for the last five parties.

You don't need a social passport. You just never go back to a stat-listed room after you've been handed a curated one.

The wave

Curated everything is eating the calendar.

Supper clubs. Run clubs. Listening bars. Invite-only everything. The events got curated — the guests didn't. We're the guest side of the wave: the room, curated toward each person standing in it. Every Luma export, every Partiful list, every conference badge-file is an un-mapped room waiting to become one.

This is relational, not a knowledge graph. A knowledge graph catalogs one person's taglines; this connects people. That distinction is the product.

The product

Drop the guest list. Find your name. The cover flips.

The flow: drop any guest CSV → the analysis plays (every number parsed live from the actual file) → the Room opens: one organic mesh connected by school, craft, belief, and interest → type your name → it waves in gradient on the map → "I'm going" → Generate my passport → the cover flips open.

The techstack — why each piece, what it makes possible

Three primitives, one audit.

Butterbase — the backend of record & the agent-native control plane

The signups and passports tables hold all 193 real rows. The OpenAI-compatible AI gateway carries every model call — why-lines, magic inferences, interest distillation, cluster naming — behind one key. The whole app ships as a static bundle to the live URL with a single tool call. And the deeper point: the app was provisioned, schema'd, seeded, deployed, and will be submitted through Butterbase's MCP — the backend is operated agent-natively end to end.

Neo4j Aura — the ontology (the product's soul)

The guest list becomes a typed property graph: Person · School · Major · Activity · Belief · ValueCluster · Interest, linked by STUDIES_AT · DOES · WORKING_ON · BELIEVES · SHARES_VALUE · INTERESTED_IN · SIGNED_UP. Two decisions make it Palantir-grade:

  • The ontology gate. Exactly one write path: typed actions (ingest_person, write_value_cluster, write_interests, check_in) validated against a single zod manifest — labels, link types, and patterns allowlisted, provenance (_src, _ts, _actor) stamped on every write. An off-ontology write is unrepresentable.
  • Typed traversal. The agent never free-runs Cypher — it walks declared read templates (same-work paths, shared-value paths, neighborhoods), each returning the path itself as a receipt. Plain Cypher only; no GDS; runs on Aura Free.
RocketRide Cloud — the deployed inference pipeline

The party-passport.pipe — a portable JSON pipeline — is deployed and resident on cloud.rocketride.ai as a managed endpoint (task-token contract). The passport-inference leg routes through it, with the Butterbase gateway as verified fallback: inference is a deployed production surface, not a laptop process.

The audit — receipts, or it doesn't ship

Every claim on every passport carries a path_receipt: the actual {from, rel, to} edges the agent walked. An audit verifies every receipt edge and every recommended person exists in the live graph — the shipped set audits at zero unreceipted claims. Guards wrap every model call: a why-line may not contain a proper noun absent from its receipt; a magic inference must be an interpretive read, never a restatement, never a fact the guest didn't give. Failures are loud; nothing falls back silently.

The invisible precache — why it feels instant

Building the room for real — normalization, enrichment, clustering, 4,000+ gated edge writes — takes minutes. So it's pre-run, and the first page replays it as the analysis: every number on screen (193 guests, 41 pending, six spellings of IYA folding into one node) is parsed live from the file you actually drop, and the static build bakes the live Neo4j graph into the bundle at export. Real work, presented at the speed of theater.

The enrichment layer — reflection, honestly marked

A Luma export is thin. The enrichment pass gives the room texture: persona archetypes simulated by a swarm of small models, 193 fully unique voices (zero repeated phrasings), derived role lines (never invented employers), and interests distilled from what each person says — 164 canonical tags, 48 shared, forming the touchpoints that bridge the clouds. Every derived field is provenance-marked as derived. On a real signup form (one extra question: "what do you think it means to be creative?"), the same pipeline runs on fully real answers.
